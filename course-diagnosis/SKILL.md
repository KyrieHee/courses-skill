# Course Diagnosis

## Workflow

1. Restate the course profile: stage, grade/year, subject, textbook/syllabus, scope, deadline, and assessment.
2. Identify known evidence: scores, teacher feedback, homework, wrong answers, notes, reading level, problem-solving level, or self-report.
3. Separate facts from assumptions; ask at most 3 key questions only when needed.
4. Diagnose prerequisites, current strengths, weak points, motivation/time constraints, and likely bottlenecks.
5. Recommend a stage sequence: preview, tutoring, practice, review, exam, mistake notebook, reflection, synthesis.

## Diagnosis Rules

- For K-12, consider curriculum sequence, textbook edition, local exam style, parent/teacher constraints, and age-appropriate workload.
- For university, consider prerequisites, lecture pace, syllabus, grading weights, assignments, readings, labs/projects, and office-hour use.
- If exact textbook content matters, ask for table of contents, unit title, lesson title, syllabus, or photos rather than guessing.
- Avoid labeling a learner as "poor"; describe observable gaps and repair paths.

## Output

Return:

- Course profile and assumptions.
- Baseline estimate with confidence.
- Strengths, gaps, and risks.
- Goal and success metrics.
- Recommended pipeline.
- Immediate next action.
