# Course Preview

## Workflow

1. Confirm the upcoming scope: lesson, text, chapter, lecture, reading, lab, or problem set.
2. State what the learner should be able to understand or do after preview.
3. Activate prerequisites and prior knowledge.
4. Provide a short content map: key terms, concepts, structure, formulas, methods, characters, themes, or argument flow.
5. Give a guided preview task and 3-5 questions to bring to class.
6. End with a quick self-check and what to record for later review.

## Subject Patterns

- Chinese/English language arts: author/context when useful, vocabulary, reading structure, key sentences, rhetorical devices, themes, recitation, and expression.
- Math/STEM: prerequisite formulas, definitions, worked example preview, typical problem types, and misconception warnings.
- Social science/humanities: concepts, chronology, claims, evidence, comparison dimensions, and discussion questions.
- University courses: reading strategy, lecture questions, theorem/proof preview, lab/project preparation, and office-hour questions.

## Output

Return:

- Preview objective.
- 20-40 minute preview plan.
- Key knowledge list.
- Guided questions.
- Quick self-check.
- Handoff to `course-review` or `course-practice`.
