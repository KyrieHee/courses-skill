# Course Reflection

## Workflow

1. Review the recent cycle: planned tasks, completed tasks, evidence, scores, and learner feelings.
2. Identify what worked and what did not.
3. Diagnose causes: task design, time, difficulty, prerequisite gap, attention, feedback, motivation, or environment.
4. Choose one or two strategy adjustments for the next cycle.
5. Define a small experiment and measurable checkpoint.

## Reflection Rules

- Keep reflection specific and non-judgmental.
- Tie conclusions to evidence: completed work, scores, error patterns, notes, or observed habits.
- Prefer small changes that can be tested within one week.
- Include emotional load and motivation when relevant, especially for long exam preparation.

## Output

Return:

- Cycle summary.
- What worked.
- What blocked progress.
- Strategy adjustment.
- Next-cycle experiment.
- Checkpoint date and metric.
